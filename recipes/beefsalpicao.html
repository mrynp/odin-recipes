Beef Salpicao

Beef Salpicao is a filipino dish composed of beef, garlic, butter and a seasoning sauce. There are a lot of beef salpicao recipes online but this one is the first one I tried and it is just so good that I didn't bother trying other recipes. The cut of beef used can be interchanged with tenderloin, sirloin or prime ribeye steaks. This dish is best served with white rice, but some enjoy it with garlic rice as well.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp Minced Garlic
  • 1 tbsp Olive Oil
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • 300 g Beef Rib Eye
  • 2 tbsp Spring Onions

Sauce:

  • 1 tsp White Sugar
  • 1/3 cup Soy Sauce
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire
  • 1 tsp Chili Flakes

Steps:

  1. Cut the beef into cubes. Add salt and pepper. Let it stand for five minutes, but the longer the better.
  2. Mix the sugar, soy sauce, worcestershire and chili flakes in a bowl.
  3. Fry off the steak in a pan with olive oil and butter. Sear on one side for 2 mins on medium high. Add in the garlic and flip over. Fry for 2 mins to heat everything up.
  4. Start seasoning with the sauce mix, tablespoon by tablespoon, depending on how salty and saucy you want the dish.
  5. Add the spring onions and stir fry for an additional 4 mins until the meat is cooked to your liking
  6. Add fresh dill to garnish.
