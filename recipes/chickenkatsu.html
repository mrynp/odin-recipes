mrynp Katsu, a popular Japanese comfort food of breaded cutlets, is commonly made with chicken or pork. It is usually served with shredded cabbage, steamed rice and tonkatsu sauce.

Ingredients:

Chicken:

  • 4 Chicken Breast or Thighs
  • 2 cups All Purpose Flour
  • 3 Eggs
  • 2 tbsp Water
  • 2 cups Panko
  • 2 cups Oil
  • Salt to taste

Tonkatsu Sauce:

  • 3 tbsp Ketchup
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire
  • 1 tbsp Oyster Sauce
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp Honey or Sugar

Cabbage Slaw:

  • 1/4 head Cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 bunch Green Onion, thinly sliced
  • Salt to taste

Sesame Dressing:

  • 5 tbsp Mayonnaise (Kewpie, if available)
  • 1 tbsp Rice Vinegar
  • 2 tsp Soy Sauce
  • 1-2 tsp Sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 2 tbsp Ground Sesame Seeds
  • 2 tsp Sesame Oil

Steps:

  1. Slice the chicken into half. Pound it until it's completely even in thickness from edge to edge (about half an inch).
  2. Season it with salt and pepper, it is better to sit it in the fridge overnight.
  3. Fill one bowl or plate with flour. A different one for the eggs mixed with water. And a different one with panko.
  4. Take one chicken breast, toss it in the flour. Make sure to coat both sides completely and evenly. Then dredge it in the egg, make sure everything gets coated. And then drop it in the panko, make sure it is totally coated. Transfer it to a small sheet of parchment paper.
  5. Repeat the coating process to all chicken breast. Stack the coated chicken with a parchment paper between them.
  6. Deep fry the chicken.
  7. Mix the tonkatsu sauce ingredients together.
  8. Slice the cabbage thinly. Add some salt, and squeeze it in.
  9. Mix the sesame dressing ingredients together.
  10. Eat the chicken with the tonkatsu sauce. And, the cabbage with the sesame dressing.
