Gyudon

Gyudon is a Japanese dish consisting of a bowl of rice topped with beef and onion simmered in a mildly sweet sauce. This recipe serves four people.

Ingredients:

  • 500 g Beef Scotch Fillet, very thinly sliced (or use any thinly sliced beef)
  • 3/4 cup Dashi (skip if it's unavailable to you)
  • 1 tsp Sugar (optional)
  • 1 Brown Onion, peeled and thickly sliced

Sauce

  • 1/4 cup Soy Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Sake
  • 2 tbsp Mirin
  • 2 tsp Sugar
  • 2 sliced spring onions
  • Cooked Rice
  • 4 Soft Boiled Eggs (optional)

Steps:

  1. Combine the stock or dashi and terkiyaki sauce with the sugar (if using) in a medium saucepan and bring to the boil.
  2. Add in the onion and simmer for 2-3 minutes until the onion has softened.
  3. Add in the beef and stir until the beef is just cooked. Season with salt if necessary.
  4. Fill 4 bowls with rice, flattening the top of the rice, and then add the beef mixture on top, allowing a little of the stock to soak into the rice. Sprinkle with spring onions, and add soft boiled egg to the bowls if you like.
